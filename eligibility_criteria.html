Digital India Excellence Awards 2017 Categories



The Digital India Excellence Awards 2017 recognize and celebrate the contributions made to the evolving and developing Digital Industry in India. Companies, NGOs, government organisations from pan-India are welcome to enter wide range of categories covering broad aspects of the Digital India. These awards encourage and support entries which grow and enhance the digital eco-system and advance the value of digital communications with noticeable benefits to the industry.

1. Citizen Services

Initiatives in the area of e-Governance through the effective use of ICT. Focus should be on how different e-governance projects have led to significant cost savings and also arresting revenue leakages in different government departments whether state or central. Projects like Passport Sewa, Ujjawala, GSTN, UIDAI and others will fall under this category.

2. Financial Services

India has seen many digital payment solutions deployment but the focus is on how these solutions have made significant contribution in terms of adoption, number of transactions and ease of use using ICT. Using the App, the monthly growth in terms of number of transactions for digital payment services and how it has helped in India moving towards a less cash economy. Innovative projects like Jan Dhan, Paytm, RuPay, etc will fall under this category.

3. Education

Focus is on innovative and sustainable technologies developed to improve content, design, delivery and assessment of academic learning both in school education as well as higher education through effective use of ICT tools. Organisations like Navodaya Vidyalaya Samiti, NCERT projects, NPTEL project of IITs/IISc, NIIT etc will fall under this category.

4. Healthcare

Initiatives taken by government players where innovative and sustainable ICT technologies have been developed to make healthcare facilities easily accessible at reasonable prices to both rural and urban masses. Focus can be on transformation projects, Apps, telemedicine projects and others which has a made significant contribution to the health sector.

5. Agriculture

Initiatives undertaken by the Central, State government as well as private and NGOs which has helped in increasing agriculture yield of farmers in the given region through effective use of ICT. Focus is on innovation, ease of use and its impact on farmers for a given region.

6. Electronics Manufacturing

India is focusing on electronics manufacturing in a big way both on equipment as well as consumer segments like mobile phone and its accessories. Till date, more than 90 electronics manufacturing units have set up shops in India and more manufacturing units are planning to set up their units in the country. How these electronics manufacturing units are creating job opportunities for India and also helping India to reduce its fiscal deficit. Government organisations like ECIL, BEL and others as well as private organisations are eligible to apply.

7. ICE Services

There is lot of convergence happening in the space of ICE (IT, Communications and Entertainment) and in this category we are focusing on ICE service providers who are helping India in the transformation process to become a digital and smart nation. Focus is on services provided and how is it impacting nation, state and district at large. Organisations like BSNL, MTNL, Airtel, Reliance Communications, Reliance Jio, Idea and others are eligible to participate.

Evaluation Criteria:

A detailed quantitative/objective methodology has been developed to fairly and accurately benchmark and analyse each entry on its merits. The jury shall evaluate entries based on four criteria: Innovation and Technology, Deployment and Dissemination, User/Customer Impact/Social Impact and Financial/Economic Impact. Scoring is based on a scale of 1-5. 1 being the lowest and 5 being the highest. The final awardees will be decided by the Jury Committee on basis of four criteria mentioned. Juries decision will be final.

1. Innovation and Technology (25%): The focus is on innovation and technology with respect to the solutions offered for a given problem. The focus is on uniqueness and novelty factor, maturity/stage of technology, investment made in the project and manpower resources deployed. Wherever possible provide facts and figures substantiating the claims to give jury the most comprehensive and informed view.

2. Deployment and Dissemination (25%): The focus is on parameters such as initiatives taken for deployment, challenges faced in terms of deployment, efforts initiated to popularise the program, %age of population impacted due to this program and challenges/obstacles overcome for the targeted group. Wherever possible, provide facts and figures substantiating the claims to give the jury the most comprehensive and informed view.

3. User/Customer/Social Impact (25%): The focus is on whether the project has benefited a large number of people. The focus is on parameters such as user/customer impact and societal/environmental impact. The note should explain how user or customer has been impacted for the given project and what has been the impact on society as well as environment because of this project. Wherever possible, provide facts and figures substantiating claims to give the jury the most comprehensive and informed view.

4. Financial/Economic Impact (25%): The focus is on growth in turnover, investment, throughput and economic gains due to digitisation. Would request if you can provide last year audited balance sheet of FY2016-17 of the said project to substantiate your claims. Focus should be on profitability as well as financial sustainability of the business model in the long term. Wherever possible, provide facts and figures substantiating claims to give the jury the most comprehensive and informed view.

Eligibility Criteria

1. The award is open to all - Companies, NGOs and Government organisations who are registered in India.

2. There are two categories of awards - Govt. and Corporate category (including NGOs).

3. In the Govt. category companies which are part of central government departments, state government departments, autonomous institutes and PSUs fall under

this category.

4. In Corporate Category, we have clubbed Corporates as well as NGOs. This includes all types of companies say Section 32 companies, NGOs, Limited companies, Private Limited companies, Proprietorship companies and others.

5. The award is open to all companies, Corporates, NGOs and government organisations in the broad eco-system of Digital India whereby they are using ICT (IT and Communication Technologies) for Citizen Services, Education, Healthcare, Financial Services, Agriculture, Electronics Manufacturing and ICE Services.

6. Entries must be submitted ONLINE ONLY ON OR BEFORE November 10, 2017 (11.59 PM IST).

7. Entries will only be accepted when submitted in the English language.

8. Organisations can submit multiple entries in different categories but limited to three.

9. Any additional online documents are accepted for each category in one go and not separately.

10. At the time and date of submitting an entry online entrants should confirm that the information supplied is truthful and accurate. If information is subsequently found to be inaccurate or incorrect, the entry or entries will be disqualified.

11. If entry or entries fail to meet the eligibility criteria set out, the entry or entries may be withdrawn from the awards competition. In the event of disqualification or withdrawal, corporate, NGOs and government organisations will not be notified.

12. The winners will be informed via email and will be felicitated during 5th Pan-IIM World Management Conference (December 14-16, 2017) at IIM Lucknow campus, Lucknow. Local Hospitality (stay and local travel) for the winners will be taken care by PAN-IIM World Management Conference

13. No travel and stay will be provided to the award winners by IIM for attending the award function and the winners will have to pay from their own pocket.
